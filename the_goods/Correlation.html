Correlation¶

I wish to see what is the correlation between the S&P 500, Hang Seng index and the a few companies that will be used to represent (precious) metal stocks.¶

The aim of this is to skimly assess the reduction of risk with investing in these indices.

Investing into companies can be very daunting. Yet, even if we are quite knowledgable about a certain company, they are still affected by their local macro-environment.The ideal scenario is to invest in companies with close to 0 correlation comparatively than closer to the -1 > x < 1 ends of the spectrum. Unforunately, very rarely do we find companies in the same locality to have a value close to zero, though they do exist. So rather than, search within the same fish bowl, let us look across the Pacific Ocean with the two markets from the US and Hong Kong. We will also look at the metal stock to see can that help us allivate risk.



A brief outline of the steps consist of: 1) Collecting data form online sources such as Yahoo financial data. 2) Format the data into Pandas DataFrames. 3) Conduct some initial analysis.

The corr_graph function allows the correlations to be viewed in a coloured grid

In [69]:
def corr_graph(data):
    data1 = data.values
    fig1 = plt.figure()
    ax1 = fig1.add_subplot(111)

    heatmap1 = ax1.pcolor(data1, cmap=plt.cm.RdYlGn)
    fig1.colorbar(heatmap1)

    ax1.set_xticks(np.arange(data1.shape[1]) + 0.5, minor=False)
    ax1.set_yticks(np.arange(data1.shape[0]) + 0.5, minor=False)
    ax1.invert_yaxis()
    ax1.xaxis.tick_top()
    column_labels = data.columns
    row_labels = data.index
    ax1.set_xticklabels(column_labels)
    ax1.set_yticklabels(row_labels)
    plt.xticks(rotation=90)
    heatmap1.set_clim(-1, 1)
    plt.tight_layout()
    plt.show()

It seems that the value of metal as an investment is a bad investment with this ~ 30 year span data.

As for the correlation of SP500 and the Hang Seng are both highly correlated bar the localised Hong Kong crash of 1997.



As we can see that for the most part the Hong Kong and the US stocks are correlated. This should come to no surprised as the Hong Kong dollar is based off the US dollar, so even if businesses are conducting work in varying fields, the finanical bread and butter is based from Uncle Sam's churning methods. Yet, with the opening of the Chinese mainland stock onto the Hong Kong stock exchange we can expect that there could be a divergence in the correlation. Though, as we have seen from the recent trade wars, the US economy and the Sino Economy are still heavily dependent on one another.
